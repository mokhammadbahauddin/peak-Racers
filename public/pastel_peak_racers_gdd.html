Section 01

Game Vision & Design Pillars

Everything in this document traces back to three non-negotiable pillars. If a feature conflicts with a pillar, the feature loses.

🌸
Pillar 1 — Cozy Competitive

The game must feel competitive without raising cortisol. Losing is gentle. Winning is satisfying but not smug. There are no "you suck" animations. Rubberband AI prevents blowouts. Stars reset frustration.

✨
Pillar 2 — Digitally Tactile

Every interaction must feel physically satisfying. The cars have weight. Drifts leave trails. Coins burst. Buttons depress. If a moment doesn't feel good to touch, it's unfinished. "Juice" is a first-class design concern.

🎨
Pillar 3 — Premium Aesthetic

The game must look good enough to screenshot and share. Glassmorphism UI. Soft isometric 3D. Clay-mation vehicles. The visual bar is set by the design mock-ups — engineering must match it, not approximate it.

Item Box Distribution Rules

Item boxes are placed at track design time. Placement rules (enforced by track design checklist):

Minimum 6 boxes per lap
Ensures every player encounters at least 2 boxes per lap on average.
No boxes on chicanes
Prevents item collection at high-danger moments. Reduces frustration.
Catch-up zone boxes
At least 2 boxes placed where 3rd/4th place typically runs. Enables comeback mechanics.

Three.js Rendering Pipeline

Lighting

One directional light (sun) at 45° elevation. Ambient light at 40% intensity. Per-world color temperature: Dawn Archipelago = warm (#FFE8D0), Sugar Snow = cool (#D0E8FF). No dynamic shadows on mobile low-quality preset.

Post-Processing Stack

Passes in order: SSAO (ambient occlusion, desktop only) → Bloom (threshold 0.8, mild glow on power-ups) → FXAA (anti-aliasing) → Color grading (per-world LUT). Motion blur pass: OFF by default, toggle in Settings.

LOD (Level of Detail)

High: Full geometry, all post-processing, 60FPS target. Medium: 50% poly reduction, no SSAO, FXAA only. Low: 25% poly, no post-processing, mobile preset. Auto-detected on first load, overridable in Settings.

Asset Streaming

Track geometry streamed on World Map selection (not at game boot). Vehicle assets cached after first load. Loading screen progress bar maps to: 0–30% auth, 30–70% track geometry, 70–100% textures + audio. Never shows 100% until truly ready.

Track Design Checklist (12 Points)

1. Minimum 6 item boxes per lap, no boxes in chicanes
2. At least 1 overtaking opportunity per 500m
3. No invisible walls — all boundaries visible in 3D
4. Par time achievable by base Class C vehicle with no upgrades
5. 3-star time requires at least 1 drift mini-turbo per lap
6. Track width: ≥18m on straights, ≥12m on corners
7. All 5 UX states tested (loading, empty, error, edge case)
8. Lap time verified to match between frontend display and server
9. 60FPS sustained on Medium LOD preset
10. At least 2 catch-up item box placements for 3rd/4th positions
11. World color palette validated against design token system
12. AI path nodes placed for all 3 difficulty presets

◆ Rubber-Band Philosophy: The rubber-band system must never feel like cheating. The AI should feel like it's "pushing itself harder," not teleporting. Catch-up boost applies only when the AI is behind — never in front. A player who dominates 150cc should feel unstoppable, not rubber-banded down.

AI Path System

Racing Line Nodes

Each track has a pre-authored racing line as a spline curve. AI navigates waypoints along this line. Waypoints specify: position, target speed, drift flag, item box interaction. Node density: 1 per 15m average.

Difficulty Path Variants

Three racing line variants per track: Optimal (150cc — shortest/fastest path), Standard (100cc — clean but not perfect), Casual (50cc — safe wide path, avoids walls). Variant selected at race start based on CC.

Section 15

Audio Design

Audio is half the "Digitally Tactile" pillar. Every sound must feel soft, warm, and satisfying — never jarring. The audio palette is acoustic + synthesized pastel: soft marimba, gentle chimes, warm bass, and airy synths.

◆ Audio Direction: If it would fit in a Studio Ghibli film, it belongs. If it sounds like a typical "game SFX pack," it doesn't. The car engine should sound warm and cartoonish, not realistic. The music should feel like a gentle breeze, not a hype track.

Music System

Dynamic Music Architecture

Each track has a 3-layer music stem system. Layers blend based on race context:

Layer A — Base: Always playing. Ambient melody, low energy.
Layer B — Mid: Fades in when player hits top 50% speed. Adds rhythm.
Layer C — Hype: Full intensity at top speed or with Star power-up active. Drops on collision.

Music Per World

World 1 — Dawn Archipelago: Tropical acoustic guitar, marimba, ocean ambience. BPM: 120.

World 2 — Sugar Snow Summit: Soft piano, music box, wind chimes, muffled bass. BPM: 108.

World 3 — Neon Night City: Lo-fi synthwave, warm pad, gentle bass. BPM: 140.

World 4 — Pastel Peak: Orchestral strings + synth hybrid. Epic but soft. BPM: 132.

Audio Settings

🔊 Master Volume

Slider 0–100%. Affects all audio buses. Default: 100%. Persists to localStorage.

🎵 Music Volume

Independent slider. Default: 80%. Many cozy gamers play with their own music. Must be instantly mutable.

🎮 SFX Volume

Independent slider. Default: 100%. SFX carries the tactile feel — never disable by default. Preview button plays a sample SFX.
